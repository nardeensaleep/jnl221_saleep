Egypt Building Collapses

I chose the Egypt Building Collapses dataviz project. The data for the project was collected by the Shadow Ministry of Housing and the Egyptian Initiative for Personal Rights. The dataviz used images more than one of the readerâ€™s senses, which is very effective for what this project is. It opens with a short paragraph giving context about building collapses in Egypt in English and in Arabic. Once you choose a language, it takes you to a recording of sounds of building collapses and an explanation of the recording as dataviz, in that every second represents two days. The circles showing collapses, injured, dead, and homeless families slowly gain more circles around the outside to represent each person or family. The key at the bottom shows the cause of each collapse in two categories and eight subcategories. However, adding up the number of collapses at the bottom, I constantly got 382, yet the circle says 392. This was a bit confusing because it is kind of like a pie chart that does not add up to 100%.

The key message of the dataviz project is that a lack of government regulation and bad planning leads to building collapses, which causes great harm to hundreds of people. The project is very effective in conveying this because it is very simple. The two main causes given are lack of government regulation and bad planning, which are explained when hovering over them. Additionally, they are broken down into eight specific subcategories under these two causes, and each subcategory is explained when the reader hovers over it as well. There is no other information provided, which in some ways made the project feel a bit incomplete for me since there is no article, but it also made the message very clear.

This dataviz project is definitely visceral. It is very emotional and conveys the humanity of the data they found. The sounds of the building collapses are not too hard to listen to so as not to turn away the reader, but just emotional enough to convery how sad of a situation this is. The sounds are definitely the most visceral element of the project. The picture in the background of the collapsed building also adds to what the producers of the project are trying to convey. It pulls the reader into the world the creators are discussing.
